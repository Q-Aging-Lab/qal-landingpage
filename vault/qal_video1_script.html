02 Het Mechanisme — Wat Triamcinolone Acetonide doet 0:50 – 2:30
Regie

Leg tube neer. Notitieboek open. Zakelijke toon. GFX: "Class 3 Corticosteroid." Rustig, gecontroleerd.

Script

"Triamcinolone Acetonide is a corticosteroid. It suppresses inflammation. In the right clinical context — eczema, psoriasis — it has legitimate uses.

The face is not that context.

Topical corticosteroids on facial skin, used daily over weeks and months, produce predictable documented effects:

Skin atrophy. The dermis physically thins. Collagen production is suppressed.

Steroid-induced hyperpigmentation. In darker skin tones, a documented driver of the exact problem Azelaic Acid is solving.

Telangiectasia. Permanently visible small blood vessels from a thinned dermis.

Rebound dependency. Stop the corticosteroid after extended use, and the skin flares.

[pauze — blik direct naar camera]

None of these effects are hypothetical. They are in the clinical literature. And they were being delivered to my face every single morning."

05 De Oplossing — The QAL Audit Protocol 4:30 – 6:30
Regie

Notitieboek open. GFX: "The QAL Audit Protocol". Duidelijk, stap voor stap.

Script

"This is the protocol I now run every time I pick up a compounded prescription. Three checks. Sixty seconds. Non-negotiable.

Step one: Verify the active and the concentration. Read the label before you leave the counter. Not at home. At the counter.

Step two: Check for abbreviations you did not prescribe. TA or TAC — Triamcinolone Acetonide. HCT — Hydrocortisone. BDP — Betamethasone Dipropionate. Any unrecognized abbreviation — stop and ask.

Step three: Compare against your original prescription on file. Ask the pharmacist to read every ingredient. Every concentration. Any deviation — refuse the batch.

[pauze]

You are not being difficult. You are auditing hardware that goes on your face every day. That is your right."
