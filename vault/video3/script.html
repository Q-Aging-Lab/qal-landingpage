02 Context — Waarom de FDA achteroploopt 0:45 – 2:00
Regie

Notitieboek open. Zakelijke toon. GFX: "US = OTC drug" vs "EU = cosmetic." Dit is het mechanisme achter het probleem.

Script

"Here is why this happens. In the United States, sunscreens are classified as over-the-counter drugs. Every new UV filter goes through the same approval process as a medication. Years of review. High barriers.

In the EU, sunscreens are classified as cosmetics. New filters get to market faster — without sacrificing safety, because the EU still runs rigorous evaluations. They just move more efficiently.

The result: EU formulators have access to a completely different toolkit. More photostable filters. Better UVA coverage. Filters that do not break down under intense UV radiation.

[pauze]

For someone living at UV index 11 or higher — that toolkit matters."

03 Het Avobenzone Probleem — Fotodegradatie 2:00 – 3:30
Regie

Intensiteit verhogen. Strakke blik. GFX: "Avobenzone = NOT photostable." AI b-roll van moleculen die degraderen cut in bij "breaks down." Tik op notitieboek bij de data.

Script

"The primary UVA filter in most American sunscreens is avobenzone. Globally approved, widely used — and one critical problem:

It is not photostable.

Avobenzone breaks down when exposed to UV light. The very thing it is supposed to absorb — it degrades under it. Significant breakdown within one to two hours of sun exposure.

[b-roll cut: moleculen degraderen]

US formulators try to stabilize it with octocrylene. But at UV index 11, in tropical heat, over a full day — you are working against physics.

The EU formulation I use does not rely on avobenzone as the primary filter. It uses Tinosorb S — photostable, covers both UVA and UVB, and is the most effective UV absorber at maximum concentration permitted in the EU.

Tinosorb S has been under FDA review for nearly two decades. Still not approved in the US."

05 UVA en Huidveroudering — Waarom UVA-PF telt 5:00 – 6:30
Regie

Leun iets naar voren. Dit is het meest relevante punt voor de doelgroep. GFX: UVA vs UVB penetratiediepte visualisatie. AI b-roll van UVA penetratie in huid cut in.

Script

"There is one more thing the US sunscreen label does not tell you.

The US uses a binary label: Broad Spectrum. Either you have it or you don't. No scale.

The EU uses a UVA-PF system — an actual number that tells you how much UVA protection you are getting. The LRP UVMune 400 I use alongside CeraVe AM has a UVA-PF of 41. That is a specific, measurable number.

Why does this matter for aging?

UVB causes sunburn. UVA causes aging. UVA penetrates deeper into the dermis — it breaks down collagen, drives hyperpigmentation, and accelerates photoaging. And UVA is present all day, through clouds, through windows, at UV index 11.

[pauze]

A sunscreen that protects you from UVB but fails on UVA is protecting you from pain — not from aging. And aging is the problem I am trying to solve."
