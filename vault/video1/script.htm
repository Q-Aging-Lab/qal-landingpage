04 Engineering Probleem — Pilling oplossen 3:30 – 5:30
Regie

Handen rustend op bureau. Rustige, gecontroleerde toon. Blader één pagina om in notitieboek bij "I had to figure this out myself". Wijs de pagina aan. B-roll van CeraVe AM + LRP UVMune naast elkaar cut in tijdens de oplossing.

Script

"Now here is something nobody talks about — and I had to figure this out myself.

[blader pagina om in notitieboek]

When you layer strong actives in the morning and then apply sunscreen on top, the products can roll off your skin in little white flakes. You lose your UV protection without even knowing it.

I call this the pilling problem. And in the tropics, with the heat and the humidity, it is worse.

The solution I found: the Azelaic Acid I use comes in a specific cream base — the kind my local pharmacy compounds. This base absorbs fully into the skin before I apply anything on top.

Once that is absorbed, I apply CeraVe AM SPF 50 first, then La Roche-Posay UVMune 400 Tinted on top.

These two layers bond properly. No rolling. No flaking. Full UV protection — which in a UV index 11 environment is not optional."
